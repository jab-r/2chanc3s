How to Use 2chanc3s

2chanc3s is a location-based platform for posting and discovering messages in your area. This guide covers the main features.

Setting Your Location

Before browsing or searching, you need to set a location. There are two ways:

Option 1: GPS (Use My Location)

Click the "Use My Location" button to use your device's GPS.

  • Your browser will ask for permission to access location
  • Works best on mobile devices
  • Accuracy varies (shown as ¬± meters)
Tip: GPS is more accurate outdoors. If you're inside, you might get better results using an address.

Option 2: Enter an Address

Type an address, city, or landmark in the address field and click "Go".

  • Works anywhere - no GPS needed
  • Great for exploring other areas
  • Examples: "Boston, MA", "Findlay Market", "123 Main St"

Adjusting the Radius

Use the "k" slider to control how far you want to see:

k valueApproximate area
1-3Immediate neighborhood
4-7District / several miles
8-15Metro area / city-wide

Hashtag Search

Prefix with # to search by hashtag:

#food - Find all posts tagged with #food
#outdoors #hiking - Find posts with both hashtags

Hashtags are case-insensitive: #Food and #food find the same posts.

Mention Search

Prefix with @ to find posts mentioning a user:

@jon - Find posts that mention @jon

Mentions are case-sensitive: @Jon and @jon are different.

Text Search

Browsing the Feed

After setting a location, posts from nearby will load automatically. The feed shows:

  • Most recent posts first
  • Posts within your selected radius (k value)
  • Username, time, and content for each post
  • Images and videos if attached

Interacting with Posts

  • Reply: Click "Reply" to open a direct message to the poster
  • Map: Click "Map" to see the post's location
  • QR Code: Some posts have QR codes for easy sharing

Tips

  • Use specific hashtags in your posts to make them easier to find
  • Mention other users with @username to get their attention
  • The more specific your location, the better your results
  • Adjust the limit slider to see more or fewer posts at once
